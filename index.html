Stacey Abrams
A Champion of Voting Equality

Stacy Abrams campaigns to be governor of Georgia.

Timeline of Stacey Abrams' Life:

  • 1973 - Abrams was born December 9th in Madison Wisconsin. She was raised in Gulf Port, Mississippi.

  • 1995 - She graduated from Spelman College, magna cum laude.

  • 1998 - Abrams studied public policy at the University of Texas Austin, earning a Masters of Public Affairs.

  • 1999 - She graduated from Yale Law School and began working as a tax attorney.

  • 2002 - Abrams began working as Deputy City Attorney for Atlanta.

  • 2007-2017 - Serving as a Representative for the State of Georgia, Abrams championed much reform including, limiting tax increases, preserving the HOPE scholorship, advocating for criminal justice reform, and expanding the transportation budget to improve logistics.

  • 2018 - Abrams began her epic gubernatorial race in Georgia, becoming the first black woman in U.S. history to be nominated by a major party for governor.

  • 2020 - After a controversial gubernatorial race in which Brian Kemp was declared the winner, Abrams founded Fair Fight 2020 to build voter protection measures.

  • 2020-2021 - Credited with her work through Fair Fight, Abrams was revered after Democratic nominee Joe Biden won the Presidential election against Donald Trump. Notably, Biden won in Georgia, a traditionally Republican state, as did Democratic Senate nominees Raphael Warnock and Jon Osoff.

  • 2021 - Abrams is nominated for a Nobel Peace Prize for her nonviolent advocacy of fair voting.
